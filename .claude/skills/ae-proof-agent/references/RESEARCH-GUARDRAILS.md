# Research Guardrails

## Objective

Produce work that is useful for diligence and product planning without contaminating the record with guesswork.

## Non-Negotiable Rules

- Do not present an inference as a fact.
- Do not invent backend details, infrastructure choices, or customer metrics.
- Do not use vague time references when exact dates matter.
- Do not say `flawless`, `simple`, or `easy to build` without showing the basis for that claim.
- If a source is unavailable, say so.
- If the evidence is mixed, say so.

## Required Output Pattern

Every substantial section should make clear which content is:
- `Verified`
- `Inference`
- `Unknown`

## Required Evidence Hygiene

- Prefer primary sources.
- Cite exact URLs.
- Record the date the source was checked.
- Separate public evidence from opinion and recommendation.
- Keep a list of management questions when information is missing.

## Red-Team Standard

Every deliverable must explicitly answer:
- What would make this thesis wrong?
- What hidden operational complexity is likely being underestimated?
- What part of the moat is actually just execution quality?
- What could a small focused team replicate in 60 to 90 days?

## Deliverable Minimum Bar

A deliverable is not acceptable unless it:
- states the product thesis clearly
- states the main red flags clearly
- distinguishes MVP feasibility from production parity
- lists key unknowns that block confidence
- proposes concrete next diligence steps
